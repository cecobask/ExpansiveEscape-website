This is a college project. This website was made by Tsvetoslav Dimov, Conor Rea and Damian Mamak. They're students in Waterford Institute of Technology, enrolled in the course BSc in Software Systems Development. This project is for the module User Experience Design, with lecturer Dr. Anne Gannon. The topic of this website is Waterford's answer to The Wild Atlantic Way. Here follows more details on what exactly WAW is:

The Wild Atlantic Way is a new and exciting tourism project in Ireland. It is, at its core, a driving route of unprecedented length, tracing 2,400 km of Irelandâ€™s western seaboard. Along the way, the drivers encounter the rugged and unspoilt coastline, epic panoramas and independent villages that have maintained their Gaelic heritage and language.

The driving route is has been launch in March 2014 and is complete with WWW road signs, its connect from Donegal in the north to Cork in the south. But this route is about much more than driving. It will link destinations and attractions, giving visitors the chance to explore the land, engage in outdoor sports and bask in traditional culture. The route links with walking paths, cycling routes discovery points, giving drivers as many reasons to get out of the car as they have to drive on.
