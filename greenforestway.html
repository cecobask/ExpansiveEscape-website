Open for visitors every day!

This trail starts in the car park and is an ideal outing for all the family. It’s a very pleasant walk through the Irish woods, perfect for families with children/couples. Towards the end of this trail, one comes to a striking piece of sculpture called the “BLACK WOLF” which symbolises a petrified tree trunk from an ancient Carboniferous forest. This is a loop walk and can be walked in either direction

Along the way, you'll come across the Forest Castle ruins. The la Poer family built the Forest castle in the early 1200’s. There is some evidence of an earlier Celtic fort on the hilltop also. The impressive silhouette comprises only about half a fifteenth century tower with bits of outer walls dating to the early thirteenth century.

Getting there: From Corofin, take the R476 and the first right onto the R460. Go left for Dysert Castle and follow the first right to Blathmac’s Church.

GPS coordinates: 53.007192,-6.34675

Suitability:

Suitable for everyone. Families with their children and couples are seen there often.

Time: 2.5 hours

Distance: 8km.
